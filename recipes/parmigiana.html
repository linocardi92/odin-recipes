Parmigiana

Description

The parmigiana di melanzane is a classic neapolitan recipe which has aubergines as its base.
There are a lot of variations of this recipe but I purpose the classic one;
for example there is the sorrentina's version in which the aubergines are breaded with eggs, flour and breadcrumbs.

Ingredients

  • Aubergines: 3
  • Mozzarella: 250 gr
  • Tomato souce: 700 gr
  • Garlic: 2 cloves
  • Basil: 4 leaves
  • Parmesan: 50 gr
  • Olive oil: 4 spoons
  • salt: to taste

Steps

  1. First clean and cut the aubergines into 0.5 cm thick slices
  2. Arrange the slices in a large bowl by seasoning each layer with salt (one tablespoon)
  3. After one hour press the aubergines to let out excess water
  4. Fry the aubergines in plenty of oil and at the same time fry the two cloves of garlic
    in a pan without letting them brown too much.
  5. Put the tomato souce in the pan with the garlic and let it cook for 10 minutes until it thickens
    then turn off the heat; add basil and salt
  6. Grease a baking dish and arrange a first layer of eggplants, side by side, without overlapping
  7. Pour two tablespoons of sauce over the eggplant and spread it evenly with the back of a spoon.
    Sprinkle with a little Parmesan cheese, but don't overdo it
  8. Cut the mozzarella into very thin slices and arrange a few on top of the sauce, well spaced apart.
    Repeat until all the ingredients are used.
  9. Finish with a generous layer of sauce, without adding any additional cheese, and a generous drizzle of olive oil.
  10. Bake the eggplant parmigiana at 200°C (400°F) for about 1 hour
    Serve the eggplant parmigiana warm or cold.
